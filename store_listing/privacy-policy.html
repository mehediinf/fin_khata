Privacy Policy — FinKhata (Smart Hisab)

Last updated: July 19, 2026 (crash reporting disclosure added)

FinKhata (in-app name "Smart Hisab") is a personal and small-business finance manager app developed by Arivon ("we", "us"). This policy explains what happens to your data when you use the app on Android or iOS.

1. Local-first design

FinKhata is a local-first application. All financial data you enter — accounts, transactions, categories, budgets, customers, suppliers, sales, purchases, and payments — is stored only in an encrypted local database on your own device. FinKhata does not have a backend server, and we do not receive, store, or have access to any of your financial data.

2. Information we do not collect

Other than the crash reporting described in Section 3, FinKhata does not include analytics, advertising, or any other third-party tracking. Specifically, the app does not:

  • Create a user account or require sign-in
  • Send your financial data (accounts, transactions, budgets, customer or supplier records) to any server operated by us or anyone else
  • Read your phone's contact list (customer/supplier names and phone numbers are typed in manually by you, and stay in the local database)
  • Track your location
  • Show advertisements
  • Sell or share your data with third parties

3. Crash reporting

FinKhata uses Firebase Crashlytics (a service provided by Google) to learn when the app crashes or hits an unexpected error, so we can fix bugs. When a crash happens, Crashlytics automatically sends Google a crash report containing technical information such as the stack trace of the error, app version, and device model/OS version. It does not receive your financial data, PIN, backups, or any content you type into the app — Crashlytics only sees the technical details of the crash itself, not your data. Crash reporting is disabled while the app is being developed/tested and is only active in the version you install from the app store. Firebase's own privacy practices are described at firebase.google.com/support/privacy.

4. App lock (PIN and biometrics)

If you set an app-lock PIN, it is never stored as plain text. Only a salted cryptographic hash of the PIN is kept in your device's secure storage, and the PIN itself cannot be recovered from it. If you enable biometric unlock (fingerprint or face), the biometric scan itself is handled entirely by your device's operating system — FinKhata never receives or stores biometric data.

5. Encrypted backups

FinKhata lets you export a workspace backup protected by a passphrase you choose (AES-256-GCM encryption). This encrypted backup is created and stays on your device until you choose to copy, save, or share it yourself. We do not receive a copy of your backups. If you lose the passphrase, the backup cannot be decrypted by us or by anyone else — please store it somewhere safe.

6. Permissions

FinKhata requests the biometric permission (Android USE_BIOMETRIC, iOS Face ID usage) solely to unlock the app on your device. No other sensitive device permissions are requested.

7. Data deletion

Since all data lives on your device, uninstalling the app — or clearing its storage from your device settings — permanently deletes it. There is no server-side copy for us to delete, because none exists.

8. Children's privacy

FinKhata is a general-audience finance tool and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

9. Future changes

If FinKhata later introduces optional cloud sync or account features, this policy will be updated in advance to describe exactly what data would be transmitted and how it would be protected, and such features will remain optional.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will reflect the latest revision.
